Sanskrit sentences typically follow a Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) structure, though the order can be flexible due to its rich case system.

रामः फलं खादति।

(Rāmaḥ phalaṁ khādati.) - Rama eats a fruit.

Sanskrit Sentence Structure

Basic Components

A simple Sanskrit sentence typically contains these elements:

1. Subject (कर्ता - kartā)

The doer of the action, usually in nominative case.

रामः (Rāmaḥ)

Rama (the subject)

2. Verb (क्रिया - kriyā)

The action word, conjugated according to person and number.

खादति (khādati)

eats (3rd person singular)

रामः खादति।

(Rāmaḥ khādati.) - Rama eats.

Structure: Subject + Verb

Sanskrit Sentence Examples

Common Sentence Patterns

Intransitive Sentences (No object)

बालः पठति।

(Bālaḥ paṭhati.) - The boy reads.

Transitive Sentences (With object)

बालः पुस्तकं पठति।

(Bālaḥ pustakaṁ paṭhati.) - The boy reads a book.

Sentences with Adjectives

सुन्दरः बालः पठति।

(Sundaraḥ bālaḥ paṭhati.) - The beautiful boy reads.

Negative Sentences

बालः न पठति।

(Bālaḥ na paṭhati.) - The boy does not read.
